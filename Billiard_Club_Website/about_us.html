About us

Come visit The Billiard Club located at Polskiego Czerwonego Krzyża 6, Rzeszów! Whether you're a casual or competitive pool player, dart, football, or shuffleboard player, The Billiard Club will make sure you have everything you need when it comes time to play.

The Billiard Shop has the largest selection of pool tables and pool cues in Eastern Canada. We deal with only quality and top industry manufacturers like; Brunswick, Legacy, Majestic and Palason pool tables - cues by Predator, Pechauer, OB, Lucasi Hybrid, Viking, Poison, Players, Action, Voodoo, Elite and Dufferin to name a few- foosball tables by Garlando and Longoni - and a huge selection of dart supplies by Unicorn, Harrows, Cosmo, Winmau, Nodor and more.

Other than billiards we carry a selection of foosball (soccer) tables, hockey tables, table tennis tables, shuffleboard tables, poker tables, and all the accessories and extras to complete your game. To help you create the perfect gaming environment, we also carry quality bar stools and chairs from Trica, Brunswick and Legacy Billiards along with a selection of elegant lighting options by RAM Gameroom and Z-Lite Lighting.

We provide competitively priced pool table installations and service work to all brands like: Palason, Legacy, Brunswick, Canada Billiard and Dufferin just to name a few. Along with cue maintenance services with our professional lathe, we work with the top tips on the market; Moori, Kamui, Sarin, Tiger, Predator, Sniper and more. Read all about it by selecting 'Our Services' from the menu above. Select 'Quick Answers' to see a complete list of answers to the most common pool table and pool cue related questions. Come and see us in person or click and see what we have to offer!

Yurii Shchehliuk

The dominant billiard game in Britain from about 1770 until the 1920’s was English Billiards, played with three balls and six pockets on a large rectangular table. The British billiard tradition is carried on today primarily through the game of snooker, a complex and colorful game combining offensive and defensive aspects and played on the same equipment as English Billiards but with 22 balls instead of three. The British appetite for snooker is approached only by the American passion for baseball; it is possible to see a snooker competition every day in Britain.
